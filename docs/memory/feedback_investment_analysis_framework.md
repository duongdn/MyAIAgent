Do not describe a business — analyze an investment. The difference:

**Description (wrong):** "Revenue tăng mạnh, nhân lực tốt, thị trường lớn"
**Analysis (right):** "Revenue growth đến từ đâu? headcount hay ARPU? Margin có mở rộng? FCF có đi cùng lợi nhuận? ROIC có giữ được?"

**Why:** Without quality-of-growth analysis, bài làm chỉ là mô tả doanh nghiệp, chưa phải phân tích đầu tư.

**How to apply:** Với bất kỳ công ty nào, bắt buộc có section về:
1. Revenue growth drivers (tăng khách / tăng giá / tăng headcount)
2. Margin trend (mở rộng hay co lại)
3. FCF vs Net Income (cash flow có thật không)
4. ROIC trend (hiệu quả sử dụng vốn có giữ được không)

**Other corrections from FPT exercise (2026-06-22):**

- FRT (FPT Retail) = separate listed company (mã FRT HOSE), FPT Corp holds ~45%. Do NOT treat as FPT's operational segment the same way as Software/Telecom. Giving too much space to FRT makes the analysis méo (distorted).

- CMMI Level 5 ≠ primary moat. It's a useful enterprise RFP entry signal, but real moat = account relationships + domain knowledge + delivery capacity + local presence. Don't overstate certification.

- Japan moat is formidable but not impenetrable. Indian vendors (TCS, Infosys) expanding in Japan. Domestic Japanese IT vendors also transforming. Avoid "bất khả xâm phạm" narrative.

- Numbers need sources and vintage year. Market share, segment revenue/profit split, headcount, growth rates — always note where these come from and when.

**Conclusion framework for Excellent Companies:**
Instead of "Good Company → wait for PE X" write:
"Excellent Company — but answer 3 questions before buying:
1. [Key uncertainty about the business model]
2. [Key uncertainty about growth drivers]
3. [Key uncertainty about valuation justification]"
